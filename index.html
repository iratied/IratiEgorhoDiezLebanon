Lebanon?

A tiny country of 6 million people located on the Mediterranean Sea which borders Syria, Israel, and occupied Palestinian Territories. Since 2011, Lebanon has taken in more than one milllion syrian refugees, as well as many others from Iraq and Palestine.

In November of 2017, Saad Hariri, Lebanon’s Prime Minister, went to Saudi Arabia.
Chaos ensued—more or less.
Hariri ended up being kept as a hostage in the KSA and made a forced resignation from his post as PM via a televised address to the Lebanese people.
There was going to be war; This pseudo-kidnapping was Saudi Arabia’s way of begin a proxy war with Iran, all to be fought out in Lebanon. The Saudi’s target was Hezbollah, a Lebanese Shia militia and political party supported by Iran and Syria.
Saudi Arabia ordered its citizens to leave Lebanon.
Panic! Pack your bags! Lebanon is going to war! These are the responses I would have expected. Instead, there was nothing. He was kept in the KSA for 3 weeks and at no moment in that span of nearly a month did anyone speak of it. I was stupefied.
What I failed to realize was that I was in Lebanon.
